August into December at the Holiday Warehouse

Posted on September 11, 2016 in 

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8449/28674407714_ef688b56a7_z.jpg" alt="Mr. Pumpkin">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Mr. Pumpkin — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28674407714/" title="Mr. Pumpkin">Flickr</a></figcaption>

The Holiday Warehouse is a largish, and rather outstanding, independent holiday decor shop located in Plano, Texas. Like its well known cousin The Decorator's Warehouse in Arlington, the concentration is mainly on Christmas, but not as exclusively.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8327/29296840235_fe9a75529a_z.jpg" alt="Veiled Doll">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Veiled Doll — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29296840235/" title="Veiled Doll">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Past rooms full of Santas, red ribbon, and nativities is the deep orange and pumpkin be-decked fall room, and finally the little section reserved for the Halloween stock, the inclusion of which sets this place apart from the store in Arlington.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8119/29188948242_0df7712f7d_z.jpg" alt="The Dark Lady">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">The Dark Lady — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29188948242/" title="The Dark Lady">Flickr</a></figcaption>

This dark lady stands sentinel outside the door, promising delights both elegant and shadow-veiled.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8546/28676041883_e6eff3c39b_z.jpg" alt="Skull Pumpkin">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Skull Pumpkin — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676041883/" title="Skull Pumpkin">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Calling back to my favorite abandoned blog of all time, this fellow both teased and excited me, as I have wanted to do something similar as a jack o’ lantern carving for many years now. Perhaps this will be the year.

<img src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7773/28676046163_4f7058aa60_z.jpg" alt="Bride and Groom">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Bride and Groom — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676046163/" title="Bride and Groom">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Skeletons are always a strong theme for Halloween, as they remind us of death and mortality. So the juxtaposition of those elements with the nuptial symbols here, signs of youth and fertility, lend this piece a certain poignancy, don't you think?

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8532/28676045173_efe36f05f2_z.jpg" alt="Bling Skull">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Bling Skull — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676045173/" title="Bling Skull">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Holiday Warehouse considers itself both “upscale” and “aspirational”, so this bling-y skull seems about right. Though I've seen similar gaudy items at Michaels and At Home this year. It's a theme for 2016. Because of Trump?

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8212/28674408514_7f23f8ef17_z.jpg" alt="No Pictures">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">No Pictures — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28674408514/" title="No Pictures">Flickr</a></figcaption>

This guy was either telling me to get my camera out of his face and move along, or else setting up for a high five. I couldn't decide.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8084/28674413064_ce9265127f_z.jpg" alt="Skeleton Party Dress">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Skeleton Party Dress — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28674413064/" title="Skeleton Party Dress">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Truthfully there were many items that looked appropriate for some sort of elaborate New Orleans masquerade ball. Made me wonder how many such parties there are in Plano come October.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8388/28676047713_7a2e7ac815_z.jpg" alt="Vampire">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Vampire — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676047713/" title="Vampire">Flickr</a></figcaption>

This vampire, high up on a top shelf, was of that ilk. With his little silk vest and lace trim, if this dapper guy wasn't Dracula, then presumably he was the Count of something.

<img src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7502/29296838735_57575a03e2_z.jpg" alt="Undead Girl">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Undead Girl — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29296838735/" title="Undead Girl">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Don't make the mistake of backing into this little girl, as I did. Because whipping around to find this staring at you can be a pit of a shock. This is the kind of thing that would feel right at home at the old Collector's Crypt. It's a good example of the variety of stock carried by the Holiday Warehouse.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8808/29263058606_70b5cb5e5a_z.jpg" alt="Zombie Face">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Zombie Face — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29263058606/" title="Zombie Face">Flickr</a></figcaption>

As is this weird guy, who I caught staring at me from a basket of hanging door decorations.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8403/28676048583_2b95461983_z.jpg" alt="Los Muertos">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Los Muertos — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676048583/" title="Los Muertos">Flickr</a></figcaption>

The Mexican holiday of Los Dias de los Muertos is gaining visibility in the States the past few years, as it rightfully should. People see it as exotic and macabre, but it's nothing more than the traditional observance of the Catholic feasts of All Saints and All Souls, albeit with a Mexican flare.

<img src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7736/28676049393_d2b9fa72ce_z.jpg" alt="Los Muertos">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Los Muertos — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676049393/" title="Los Muertos">Flickr</a></figcaption>

The calaveras, the candied skulls and brightly decorated skeletons are a reminder of the transitory nature of human life, and of the love we always share with our dearly departed. The fear of death, and its ugliness, is transformed into love and beauty through the redemptive power of Christ's resurrection, in which the faithful hope to share. It's far from morbid. These little statues had a certain grace, fine detail, and surprisingly not too expensive.

<img src="https://farm8.staticflickr.com/7645/29218355901_928fb7ce4e_z.jpg" alt="Skulls">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Skulls — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218355901/" title="Skulls">Flickr</a></figcaption>

More on the meditative end of the spectrum were these stacked skulls, which I may yet walk away with as a piece for the fireplace.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8440/29218357161_9184316b0a_z.jpg" alt="Graveyard">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Graveyard — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218357161/" title="Graveyard">Flickr</a></figcaption>

These little cemetery scenes caught my eye for their intricate detail and strange symbolism. It the dog on the altar had been a bulldog, I would have bought it on the spot.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8096/29218359741_257397c6b1_z.jpg" alt="Graveyard Altar">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Graveyard Altar — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218359741/" title="Graveyard Altar">Flickr</a></figcaption>

The place does fancy really well, but there's also rustic here and there, like these artificially weathered old yard signs, which I have to admit look pretty cool.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8273/29218367461_6976c569cd_z.jpg" alt="Pumpkin Witch">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Pumpkin Witch — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218367461/" title="Pumpkin Witch">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Did I somebody say fancy? This healthy looking pumpkin witch is all set to hand out candy at your next Halloween costume ball, and maybe a couple of steins of beer as well.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8471/29218372251_78bf1c618b_z.jpg" alt="Bowl of Skulls">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Bowl of Skulls — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218372251/" title="Bowl of Skulls">Flickr</a></figcaption>

I told you bling-y skulls were in this year. But what's better than a bling-y skull? A bowlful of elaborately embossed, authentic brass skulls, of course.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8230/29263060166_10888487d8_z.jpg" alt="Gargoyles">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Gargoyles — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29263060166/" title="Gargoyles">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Gargoyles are a Halloween theme that need to make a comeback, in my opinion. When I was a kid I remember gargoyles being a much bigger deal. But I watched a lot of Scooby Doo.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8043/28674405584_a051684a32_z.jpg" alt="Mr. Pumpkin">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Mr. Pumpkin — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28674405584/" title="Mr. Pumpkin">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Back out in the hall I had a stop and gawk at Mr. Pumpkin for a bit. It's a little hard to tell from the photograph, but this thing must stand like six feet tall. And I can only imagine that the cost of all his fall-flavored finery must be many times that of the nicest suit I've ever worn. He was exquisite, that guy.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8200/28676039923_b4c5dba8f4_z.jpg" alt="Tom Turkey">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Tom Turkey — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/28676039923/" title="Tom Turkey">Flickr</a></figcaption>

And lest you think old Tom Turkey gets slighted, forget it. He was there in many manifestations, in all his November glory. This particular model cost over a thousand dollars and stood about three feet tall. He was obviously some sort of table centerpiece and looked so grand I had to take a selfie with him. (Selfie not shown).

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8087/29218423951_2d77dfefec_z.jpg" alt="Made in Italy">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Made in Italy — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218423951/" title="Made in Italy">Flickr</a></figcaption>

On the way back out of the store we traveled quickly from November to December, where we stopped for a minute to admire the selection of enormous and truly beautiful nativity sets on display.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8156/29218425201_25033efb9f_z.jpg" alt="Fontanini">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Fontanini — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218425201/" title="Fontanini">Flickr</a></figcaption>

We were informed that the Pope owns this very set, and displays it at the Vatican in Rome. I'm not sure which pope, but presumably Pope Francis. Honestly, it looked good enough.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8874/29218418251_09fcc2aee2_z.jpg" alt="Wise Man">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Wise Man — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218418251/" title="Wise Man">Flickr</a></figcaption>

The figures were all incredibly life-like, and bore varying expressions of nobility, joy, and a kind of worshipful purpose.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8087/29218418941_de8ccfc62a_z.jpg" alt="Balthasar">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Balthasar — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218418941/" title="Balthasar">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Golden eyes were a strange, confronting choice for old Balthasar! But it works. The statue's gaze is very intense, and overall the impression is very life-like.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8192/29218421581_0176847125_z.jpg" alt="St. Joseph">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">St. Joseph — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218421581/" title="St. Joseph">Flickr</a></figcaption>

St. Joseph gazes down in thoughtful adoration…

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8321/29218420951_2a0624c15a_z.jpg" alt="The Virgin Mary">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">The Virgin Mary — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218420951/" title="The Virgin Mary">Flickr</a></figcaption>

…while the Virgin Mary seems enraptured at the sight of the baby Jesus. So well done, so well crafted.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8587/29218419901_a152f8b508_z.jpg" alt="Baby Jesus">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">Baby Jesus — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218419901/" title="Baby Jesus">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Little baby Jesus seemed the weakest part of the set, unfortunately. His head was a little too small. His arms and legs are all out of proportion. It's meant to be artistically devotional, but it should have been a tad more lifelike, in my opinion.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8175/29218422781_f7fd7cb615_z.jpg" alt="The Blessed Virgin Mary">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">The Blessed Virgin Mary — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218422781/" title="The Blessed Virgin Mary">Flickr</a></figcaption>

A different set, with a strikingly beautiful representation of the Blessed Virgin. The cradle on that day was vacant, filled only with decorative shrubbery. Too bad. Would have liked to have seen more of this set.

<img src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8517/29218426471_1229bc7d68_z.jpg" alt="The Living Tree">
<figcaption class="wp-caption-text">The Living Tree — Photo by <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/">Tobyblog</a> on <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/tobyjmarks/29218426471/" title="The Living Tree">Flickr</a></figcaption>

Goodbye for now! Hope reading through all this has given you a little taste of holiday spirit, courtesy of the Holiday Warehouse. Like what you've seen? Here's a link to the online store. More to come.
